Phoebe's Story

Phoebe Dawson was a ray of sunshine—a beloved member of Sydney's North Shore and Northern Beaches community whose warmth, compassion, and genuine care for others touched everyone who knew her. Her infectious smile and kind spirit made her impossible to forget.

Phoebe dedicated her career to caring for others as a nurse, first as an Oncology Nurse at Royal North Shore Hospital, where she provided comfort and hope to patients during their most vulnerable moments. She later brought that same dedication to the local community as a nurse at South Steyne Medical Centre in Manly, where she became a familiar and cherished face to countless locals.

During the COVID-19 pandemic, if there were any Northern Beaches locals who hadn't met our girl yet, they certainly did when Phoebe served as one of the main nurses administering COVID tests and vaccinations. Through it all, she brought her characteristic warmth and reassurance to everyone she cared for.

Tragically, Phoebe lost her life in February 2024 while driving in a storm. The loss of our sunshine girl has left a void in the hearts of all who knew her, but her legacy of compassion, service, and joy continues to inspire us.

The Phoebe Dawson Foundation was established by her husband Tim and their family to ensure that Phoebe's radiant spirit continues to make a difference. In everything we do, we ask ourselves: What Would Phoebe Do? (WWPD). The answer guides us to spread kindness, support our community, and live life with the same generous heart that defined Phoebe.

A Note from James Barnet

This is an update from Phoebe's father, James Barnet, on recent developments and future plans for the Phoebe Dawson Foundation.

Recent Contributions

Cook Park, Orange

Funded the erection of a picnic table and bench adjacent to the fernery - the location where Tim proposed to Phoebe.

Little Manly

Installed a park bench at the top of the park below the carpark and looking out across the harbour - where Phoebe and Tim were married.

Bear Cottage, Manly

Donated towards a mechanised awning over the outdoor deck area, providing comfort for children and families during palliative care visits.

WWPD Events

In early February 2025, 29 intrepid swimmers participated in the Cole Classic "the world's biggest ocean swim" under the WWPD (What Would Phoebe Do) banner. All participants wore custom yellow and white swimmers with WWPD branding. The previous day, 10 participants took part in the 10km Dee Why to Manly Sun Run.

These events brought together participants of all ages and helped build awareness of the foundation's mission to support causes close to Phoebe's heart.
